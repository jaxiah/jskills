# The 20 Rules of Formulating Knowledge

Based on Piotr Wozniak's principles from SuperMemo:

1.  **Do not learn if you do not understand**
2.  **Learn before you memorize** (build an overall picture before focusing on details)
3.  **Build upon the basics**
4.  **Stick to the minimum information principle** (keep items as simple as possible)
5.  **Cloze deletion is easy and effective** (use fill-in-the-blank sentences)
6.  **Use imagery**
7.  **Use mnemonic techniques**
8.  **Graphic deletion is as good as cloze deletion** (hide parts of an image to test yourself)
9.  **Avoid sets** (avoid lists of items without a specific order)
10. **Avoid enumerations** (ordered lists are better than sets, but still difficult; use cloze deletion)
11. **Combat interference** (distinguish between similar pieces of information)
12. **Optimize wording** (make questions and answers as concise as possible)
13. **Refer to other memories** (place new information in the context of what you already know)
14. **Personalize and provide examples**
15. **Rely on emotional states** (vivid or shocking examples are easier to remember)
16. **Context cues simplify wording** (use labels or categories to provide context)
17. **Redundancy does not contradict the minimum information principle** (learning the same thing from different angles is helpful)
18. **Provide sources**
19. **Provide date stamping** (useful for volatile or time-sensitive information)
20. **Prioritize** (focus on the most important information first)
